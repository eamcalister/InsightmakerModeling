Worksheet Exercise 1.

Define variables as follows:
\begin{align*} A\amp = \amp \text{cross sectional area of a baseball} \\ m\amp = \amp \text{mass of a baseball} \\ \rho\amp = \amp \text{density of air} \\ v\amp = \amp \text{velocity of the baseball} \\ K\amp = \amp \text{a dimensionless coefficient} \end{align*}
Assume that the force acting to slow down a baseball in flight is given by
\begin{equation*} F = mv' = -KA^{a}\rho^{b}v^{c}. \end{equation*}
  1. Use dimensional analysis to explain why the exponent \(d\) must equal \(2\text{.}\)
  2. Explain why it makes sense to have the minus sign in front of \(K\text{.}\)
Hint.
Attach units to all the terms on both sides of the given equation and observe that time units only appear in one place on the right-hand side.
